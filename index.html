Access Control

Limit the physical and logical entry points so only authorized people reach sensitive systems.

Access control restricts who can enter or interact with critical assets. Protect the crown jewels first—servers and critical data—and extend controls outward through rooms, floors, and the full facility.

  • Restrict access to sensitive areas across every organization size.
  • Safeguard equipment like servers, workstations, and networking gear.
  • Protect confidential data and intellectual property.
  • Keep employees, contractors, and visitors safe.

Mantraps

One person at a time. No tailgating. Continuous verification.

A mantrap is a controlled entry chamber with two interlocking doors. Only one door opens at a time, creating an enforced pause between public and secure areas.

  1. Entry

    The first door unlocks for a single authorized person.

  2. Secure Check

    The door closes behind them while guards or sensors validate identity.

  3. Release

    If cleared, the second door opens into the secure zone.

Mantraps are ideal at transitions between lobbies and server rooms, data centers, or laboratories. They deter tailgating and provide time for identity verification.

Computer & Device Security

Lock down endpoints with layered physical and logical defenses.

Servers

  • House in locked server rooms or locking cabinets.
  • Consider bolted racks for tamper resistance.
  • Log physical access to track who enters sensitive spaces.

Desktops & Laptops

  • Use cable locks or docking stations with security slots.
  • Lock desks and storage when unattended.
  • Enforce automatic screen locks and strong authentication.

Mobile Devices

  • Require PINs, passwords, or biometrics for access.
  • Deploy Mobile Device Management (MDM) solutions such as Intune.
  • Use MDM to enforce encryption, locate lost devices, and remote wipe.

Keyloggers

  • Hardware keyloggers sit between keyboard and PC—inspect cabling.
  • Software keyloggers ride on malware—keep antivirus active and updated.
  • Wireless keyboards can be intercepted—prefer encrypted peripherals.

Removable Media

Portable storage brings flexibility—and attack surface. Control it.

Unprotected

  • Unrestricted USB/CD ports invite malware and data theft.
  • No encryption means lost drives expose sensitive files.
  • Attackers may plant rogue devices to pivot inside.

Encrypted

  • Disable or tightly manage USB and optical ports.
  • Encrypt with BitLocker or BitLocker To Go when enabled.
  • Store recovery keys separately from the media itself.

Summary / Exam Notes

Use these quick references during study sessions or pre-exam reviews.

Defense in Depth layers controls so that each barrier buys time, reduces risk, and signals attacks quickly. Pair physical measures such as fences and mantraps with logical controls like encryption and MDM policies.

Topic Key Idea Security Mechanism
Access Control Restrict access to critical assets. Keys, cards, biometrics, logging.
Site Security Stack physical protections in layers. Fences → Locks → Secure rooms.
Mantraps Stop tailgating with controlled entry. Dual-door vestibules with verification.
Device Security Protect endpoints from tampering. Locks, MDM, encryption, monitoring.
Removable Media Control data flow on portable drives. Disable ports or enforce encryption.
